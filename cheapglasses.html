The Science of Vision Correction: Addressing Presbyopia

Presbyopia is a natural age-related condition where the eye's lens loses its flexibility, making it harder to focus on nearby objects. This gradual stiffening of the eye's lens with age begins typically in one's 40s and progresses gradually. Presbyopia treatment options include bifocals, which effectively correct both near and far vision. The gradual stiffening affects the lens' ability to change shape and adjust its focus. Bifocal lenses address this loss of accommodation, enabling clear vision at both distances.
